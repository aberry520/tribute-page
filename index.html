Roman Emperor

Marcus ruled as Roman emperor from 161 until his death in 180 AD. Upon his death Marcus was succeeded by his son Commodus, whom he had previously named Caesar in 166 and with whom he had jointly ruled since 177. Much beloved by his peers and those he ruled most concider him to be he last of the rulers known as the Five Good Emperors during an age of relative peace, calmness and stability for the Roman Empire lasting from 27 BC to 180 AD..

Stoic Philosophy

Stoicism flourished throughout the Roman and Greek empires until the 3rd century AD, and among its adherents was Emperor Marcus Aurelius. Still many today practice this philosphy that has prevailed for centuries.
